Recipe for Pasta Aglio e Olio

Description

This definitely is not authentic, and may not even be considered pasta aglio e olie at all, but it's my take on a dish in its vein. One night, I had leftover fettuccine noodles, some raw spinach, garlic that was getting old, and frozen meatballs. I threw them together with a generous helping of EVOO and parmesan cheese, and this was the result. The family liked it so much I've made it countless times since, and finally wrote it down in a recipe.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb pasta (I usually use fettuccine)
  • 8-12 large garlic cloves, sliced thin (I’ve also minced it)
  • ½ cup EVOO
  • Red pepper flakes, to taste
  • Couple of grinds of salt and black pepper, to taste
  • Dried parsley flakes
  • ½ cup grated parmesan cheese
  • 24 Kirkland Italian meatballs, heated in microwave
  • 1 tub of baby spinach from Costco

Steps

  1. Cook pasta until it is slightly firmer than you normally like.
  2. Combine garlic and olive oil in a cold skillet large enough to also hold the pasta. Cook over medium heat to slowly toast garlic, about 10 minutes. Reduce heat to medium-low when garlic begins to brown slightly. Cook and stir until garlic is golden brown, at least another 5 mins.
  3. Add salt, pepper, red pepper flakes, and dried parsley flakes. Stir.
  4. Add pasta and meatballs. Toss in the oil mixture and continue to stir/toss occasionally until warm.
  5. Add baby spinach and mix until wilted.
  6. Turn off the heat. Sprinkle in parmesan cheese and toss to coat.
  7. Serve.
